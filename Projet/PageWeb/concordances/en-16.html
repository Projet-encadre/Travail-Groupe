In [111]mathematics, a root of unity, occasionally called a [112]de
Moivre number, is any [113]complex number that yields 1 whenroot
[114]raised to some positive [115]integer power n. Roots of unity areroot
used in many branches of mathematics, and are especially important inroot
[116]number theory, the theory of [117]group characters, and theroot
[118]discrete Fourier transform.root
root
Roots of unity can be defined in any [119]field. If theroot
[120]characteristic of the field is zero, the roots are complex numbers
that are also [121]algebraic integers. For fields with a positiveroot
characteristic, the roots belong to a [122]finite field, and,
[123]conversely, every nonzero element of a finite field is a root of
unity. Any [124]algebraically closed field contains exactly n nth roots
of unity, except when n is a multiple of the (positive) characteristicroot
of the field.root
