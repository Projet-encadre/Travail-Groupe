The root cap is a type of tissue at the tip of a plant
[75]root.^[76][1] It is also called [77]calyptra. Root caps contain
[78]statocytes which are involved in [79]gravity perception inroot
plants.^[80][1] If the cap is carefully removed the root will grow
randomly. The root cap protects the [81]growing tip in plants.^[82][1]
It secretes [83]mucilage to ease the movement of the root through
soil,^[84][1] and may also be involved in communication with the soilroot
microbiota.^[85][1]root
