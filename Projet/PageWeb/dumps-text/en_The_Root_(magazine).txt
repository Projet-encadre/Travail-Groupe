   The Root is an African American-oriented [62]online magazine. It was
   launched on January 28, 2008, by [63]Henry Louis Gates Jr. and
   [64]Donald E. Graham.

History

   [[65]edit]

   The Root was owned by [66]Graham Holdings Company through its online
   subsidiary, [67]The Slate Group.^[68][1]^[69][2] In 2015, Graham
   Holdings sold The Root to [70]Univision Communications.^[71][3] The
   site was subsequently re-launched under the [72]Kinja platform used by
   other [73]Gizmodo Media Group (formerly [74]Gawker Media)
   websites.^[75][4] GMG was later succeeded by [76]G/O Media as owner of
   The Root.

   In July 2017, the blog Very Smart Brothas, co-founded by [77]Damon
   Young and Panama Jackson, became [78]a vertical of The Root.^[79][5]

   [80]Danielle Belton was editor-in-chief at The Root between 2017 and
   2021, when she was appointed editor of [81]HuffPost.^[82][6]^[83][7] On
   April 14, 2021, it was announced that Vanessa De Luca had been
   appointed editor-in-chief.^[84][8] She was succeeded by Tasha Robertson
   on June 22, 2023.^[85][9]

   Since April 2021 The Root has seen substantial staff turnover with 15
   out of the 16 full-time staffers resigning following internal tensions,
   with former staff member Michael Harriot saying that, "as a staff, we
   came to the conclusion that, basically, The Root is over."^[86][10]

The Root 100

   [[87]edit]

   The Root 100 is the magazine's annual "list of the 100 most important
   black influencers between the ages of 25 and 45."^[88][11] This list
   has been published since 2011, when it began ranking honorees using an
   influence score that resulted from a mathematical formula developed by
   scholar [89]Omar Wasow.^[90][12] Readers of The Root are allowed to
   nominate those who they feel are deserving of being on this list. The
   list has had a wide variety of people from celebrities such as
   [91]Donald Glover, writers like [92]Roxane Gay, and athletes. Other
   influential people in the black community who are not as well known,
   such as activists, are encouraged to be added to the list as
   well.^[93][13]
