   Root is a Czech [79]black metal band.

Biography

   [[80]edit]

   Root were formed by vocalist [81]Big Boss and guitarist Blackie in late
   1987.^[82][1] According to Big Boss, in the beginning, they knew no
   foreign bands and could do no tape trading.^[83][2] Although the first
   demo recording mentioned on their homepage's discography section is
   Deep in Hell,^[84][3] according to their biography, they recorded their
   first official demo tape Reap of Hell after a few rehearsals.^[85][1]
   Root played their first concert in September 1988. The next demo
   cassette, War of Rats, was released in 1988, followed by Messengers
   from Darkness in 1989. Vocalist Big Boss played drums on these
   recordings.^[86][1] Whereas Messengers from Darkness is mentioned as
   "the third and last" demo cassette in their biography,^[87][1] the
   discography section lists The Trial as the last one.^[88][3]

   In 1990, the band released the single 7 cerných jezdcu / 666, with
   artwork done by [89]Master's Hammer vocalist [90]Frantisek Storm. To
   promote their first album, [91]Zjevení (again with artwork by Storm),
   they recorded a video for their song "Hrbitov", released on the Czech
   Death Metal Session compilation,^[92][1] with photographs by Storm.
   Zjevení was also released as [93]The Revelation.

   The second album, [94]Hell Symphony, followed in 1992. In 1993, Root
   released their third album, [95]The Temple in the Underworld, and
   recorded a video for the song "Aposiopesis". The following years "were
   in the sign of personal problems between two main personalities of
   ROOT, Big Boss and Blackie".^[96][1] In 1996, Root released their first
   [97]concept album, [98]Kärgeräs.^[99][1] Their fifth album, [100]The
   Book, followed in 1999.

   In 2004, Root played on the Open Hell festival with [101]Watain,
   [102]Törr and other bands.^[103][4] In 2011, they released the album
   [104]Heritage of Satan, featuring [105]Aura Noir and [106]Ava Inferi
   guitarist [107]Rune "Blasphemer" Eriksen, [108]Behemoth vocalist
   [109]Adam "Nergal" Darski and Watain vocalist Erik Danielsson, who also
   created the album cover, as guest musicians. Although Big Boss is the
   only remaining founding member, the band's core now consists of him,
   bass player Golem and guitarist Ashok.^[110][5]

   On 28 September 2016 the band announced that they began work on their
   tenth studio album, [111]Kärgeräs - Return from Oblivion, the sequel to
   their 1996 concept album Kärgeräs. It was released on 25 November
   2016.^[112][6]

Musical style and ideology

   [[113]edit]

   According to Legacy journalist Christian Wachter, Root have, since
   their beginning, a very own style (although ostensibly [114]Venom- and
   [115]Bathory-oriented) with an [116]occult aura and are one of the few
   bands deserving the term "cult".^[117][7] Portuguese band
   [118]Moonspell mentioned Root as one of their influences.^[119][8]
   [120]Rock Hard journalist Björn Thorsten Jaschinski called them "occult
   metal pioneers".^[121][9] His colleague Wolf-Rüdiger Mühlmann located
   The Book "in the triangle of [122]death metal, [123]gothic metal and
   gloomy rock" with folklore melodies that could be interesting for fans
   of [124]Katatonia, [125]My Dying Bride, [126]Primordial and
   Thine.^[127][10] On Root's homepage, Black Seal is described as a
   mixture of "the heaviness of 'Hell Symphony', monumentality of 'The
   Temple In The Underworld', mystery of 'Kargeras' and the dark charm of
   'The Book'";^[128][1] Mühlmann described it as a mix of [129]Mercyful
   Fate, Moonspell, [130]Anathema and old [131]heavy metal.^[132][11] For
   [133]Decibel journalist Chris D., Heritage of Satan "feels less
   complete than, say, The Book, which is, for me at least, the best Root
   album to date".^[134][12] The album features "a few rocking songs",
   "Revenge of Hell" and "Legacy of Ancestors", as well as "more
   traditional black metal songs".^[135][12]

   Big Boss founded the Czech branch of the [136]Church of
   Satan.^[137][7]^[138][12] He still lives his life according to
   [139]Satanism,^[140][7]^[141][12] but "gave away all agenda [...] to
   younger ones"^[142][12] and now believes that "everything that smells
   of organization is shit".^[143][12] Although Root are therefore close
   to [144]LaVeyan Satanism, [145]Watain (whose vocalist Erik Danielsson
   contributed to Heritage of Satan), who are [146]Devil
   worshippers,^[147][13] called them and [148]Törr "the legendary kings
   of Czech Black/Death Metal" and "defenders of the faith" when they
   announced they would play on the Open Hell festival in 2004.^[149][4]
   According to Big Boss, "the difference between us and Scandinavian
   groups is that I sing about Satanic [150]magic and myths I create
   personally, whereas Swedes, Norwegians etc. sing about things that are
   already known. They therefore have another approach to these
   topics."^[151][7] On Heritage of Satan, the topic is the final,
   successful assault of the infernal hordes onto the earth, after which
   [152]Satan gives His power to His son.^[153][7]

Current members

   [[155]edit]
